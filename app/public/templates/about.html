Adoption

Our visual notation is being used to develop ontologies in the following projects and is part of the suite of tools of the Linked Open Terms methodology.

  • COGITO

    COGITO is a EU funded project that aims to materialize the digitalization benefits for the construction industry by harmonizing Digital Twins with the Building Information Model concept and establishing a digital Construction 4.0 toolbox.

  • BIMERR

    BIMERR is a EU funded project which aims to provide a suite of interoperable tools to support AEC stakeholders throughout the energy efficiency renovation process of existing buildings.

  • SAREF

    The Smart Applications REFerence (SAREF) ontology is a shared model of consensus that facilitates the matching of existing assets in the smart applications domain. The following modules / extensions of the SAREF ontology were constructed using our notation: SAREF Core, SAREF4Building, SAREF4Envi, SAREF4City, SAREF4Agri, SAREF4Wear, SAREF4Water

  • DELTA

    DELTA is an EU funded project under Horizon 2020. It proposes a demand-response management platform that introduces scalability and adaptiveness into the Aggregator’s DR toolkits.

  • VICINITY

    VICINITY is an EU funded project under Horizon 2020. It proposes a platform and ecosystem that provides “interoperability as a service” for infrastructures in the Internet of Things.

  • EASYTV

    EasyTV is a H2020 European project which main goal is to provide equal access to television and audio-visual services to that all users, especially persons with disabilities and users with special needs.

  • W3C RML ontologies

    The RML Ontology Modules are being developed by W3C Community Group on Knowledge Graph Construction to define the RDF Mapping Language (RML) specification.

  • W3C WoT discovery ontology

    The WoT discovery ontology has been developed to enrich Thing Descriptions with information about their registration into a Thing Directory. To this end, the WoT discovery ontology extends the Thing Description ontology and the DID ontology. Furthermore, to ease the data ingestion along with the ontology two JSON-LD 1.1 contexts have been developed.

  • DiCon ontology network

    The Digital Construction Ontologies (DiCon) are an enabler for the semantic interoperability between systems in the construction and renovation domain built by the BIM4EEB EU project.

  • CosWOT project

    French national project aiming at proposing a distributed WoT-enabled software architecture embedded on constrained devices.

  • D2KAB project
  • Integration within Mat-o-Lab software
  • ICT infraestructure ontologies

    The ICT infraestructure ontology network was created with the purpose of serving as an initial step towards an homogeneous representation of DevOps, and which has been already used to produce a knowledge graph for a large ICT company.

  • Public transport ontologies

    This vocabulary represents the information of the urban bus public transport system in Spain. The international standard TransModel which contains the model for the exchange of transport information between companies, operators or transport authorities of the European Union member states has been reused in this ontology. According to regulation 2017/1926 which follows the European directive 2010 on intelligent transport services ITS (Intelligent Transport System) any authority, operator or company of land transport services must offer their data in formats compatible with Transmodel from December 2019.
